# PolyHarmony Core Features

The core features proposed for the polyamorous calendar app, PolyHarmony, are designed to address the unique scheduling and relational complexities faced by polyamorous users, differentiating it significantly from mainstream and even niche competitors.
Here are the core features, their benefits, and how they differentiate the app:
• Relationship-Aware Data Model / Relationship Dashboards:
    ◦ User Benefits: This is the most significant differentiator, as it shifts the app's fundamental focus from merely managing events to explicitly managing relationships. Users can define partners and the nature of their connections (e.g., nesting partner, long-distance, triad). Dedicated dashboards for each relationship can visualize key metrics like Time Equity (using heatmaps or balance wheels to represent the allocation of quality time) and offer Connection Prompts for proactive emotional care (e.g., suggesting a date night or check-ins). It also automatically tracks and reminds users of Anniversaries & Milestones for each relationship, helping prevent forgotten commitments. This transforms the app into an indispensable "System of Record" for relational life, fostering deeper connections and preventing burnout.
    ◦ Differentiation: Mainstream calendars lack the concept of "relationships" beyond basic sharing. Even group-focused tools like TimeTree and Weel lack deep, relationship-centric analytics or emotional support features, and are primarily logistical tools rather than relational enablers. PolyHarmony's underlying "Relational Graph" data model allows for features that competitors cannot easily replicate, creating a powerful "data moat" and high switching costs as users invest in defining their relationships within the app.
• Effortless Privacy Controls (Granular and Intuitive):
    ◦ User Benefits: Users consistently prioritize granular privacy, often citing "non-negotiable" needs for partners not to see each other's information unless explicitly chosen. This feature provides event-level visibility controls (e.g., showing an event as "busy" versus full details) and a "Privacy View" Toggle that allows users to instantly see their calendar as a specific partner would. Users can also create Polycule Groupings with predefined privacy rules for one-tap sharing. This directly addresses the top user frustration and relationship conflict source.
    ◦ Differentiation: Mainstream calendars typically offer calendar-level sharing, not granular event-level control, leading to accidental oversharing. PolyHarmony's privacy controls are built as a core, foundational feature, not an afterthought, and are designed to be visually intuitive, preventing accidental oversharing through smart defaults.
• AI-Powered Smart Scheduling and Conflict Resolution ("Weave AI"):
    ◦ User Benefits: The app's central innovation is the "Weave AI," a proactive scheduling engine that acts as a relational well-being coach. It enables Natural Language Event Creation (e.g., "Coffee with [Partner A] next Tuesday at 3 PM"), automatically parsing details like date, time, duration, and attendees. The AI provides automated conflict detection that not only flags overlaps but also proposes solutions, considering participant availability, meeting priorities, and user preferences. This significantly reduces the cognitive load and stress associated with complex polyamorous scheduling. Transparency logs are included in the MVP to show why an AI suggestion was made.
    ◦ Differentiation: Competitors like TimeTree and Weel, while offering some group features, lack an integrated, intelligent agent that actively works to solve scheduling conflicts and reduce user burden. They are passive tools, whereas PolyHarmony's AI acts as an "active assistant". This AI is explicitly designed for multi-person, preference-based schedule optimization, which could be patentable.
• Frictionless Onboarding & Mobile-First Design:
    ◦ User Benefits: Recognizes that partners may be reluctant to adopt new apps. The onboarding process is designed for one-click setup and progressive feature disclosure, revealing advanced features gradually to avoid overwhelming new users. It is optimized for quick, on-the-go interactions typical of mobile use. This aims to overcome major adoption barriers and enable network effects within polycules.
    ◦ Differentiation: Many apps can overwhelm users with complexity upfront. PolyHarmony's focus on simplifying the initial experience for all members of a polycule, coupled with features like voice command support for event creation, distinguishes it from general-purpose calendar apps that don't prioritize such adoption challenges for multiple users.
These core features, particularly the "Relationship-Aware" data model and the "Effortless Privacy" controls, form a unique value proposition that is purpose-built for the polyamorous community, creating a deep, defensible moat against incumbent, one-size-fits-all solutions. The Freemium Business Model supports this by offering the core scheduling and privacy features for free to encourage adoption, while the premium tier unlocks the advanced AI features and the "Relationship Dashboard". The business model also explicitly emphasizes a no-data-selling policy to build trust within this sensitive user base, further enhancing differentiation.
