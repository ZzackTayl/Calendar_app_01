# Missed Opportunities and Unconsidered Use Cases

This document outlines potential features and use cases that may not have been fully considered during the initial design of the PolyHarmony calendar application. These suggestions are based on an analysis of the project's goals and the specific needs of the polyamorous community.

## 1. Metamour and Compersion Support

*   **Metamour-Only Events:** Events specifically for metamours (partners of one's partner) to build relationships and foster a sense of community.
*   **Compersion Celebrations:** A way to acknowledge and celebrate positive feelings (compersion) for a partner's other relationships. This could be a special event type or a "kudos" feature.

## 2. Relationship Evolution and History

*   **Relationship Status Changes:** A way to log changes in relationship status (e.g., from "dating" to "committed") and have that reflected in the calendar's history. This could provide a rich, historical view of a user's relationships.
*   **Anniversary Tracking:** Tracking multiple types of anniversaries (e.g., first date, first time meeting a metamour, first time a triad went on vacation together).

## 3. Advanced Scheduling and Availability

*   **"Soft" Holds:** A way to pencil in an event without confirming it, allowing for more flexible and tentative planning.
*   **Availability Broadcasting:** A feature where users can broadcast their availability to their network or specific groups, making it easier to find times that work for everyone.
*   **Time-Sensitive Events:** Events that are only visible to certain people for a limited time (e.g., a surprise party).

## 4. Child and Dependent Management

*   **Child Profiles:** Profiles for children or other dependents, with their own schedules and activities.
*   **Co-Parenting Schedules:** Tools for managing complex co-parenting arrangements between multiple adults, including custody schedules, school events, and medical appointments.
*   **Caregiver Permissions:** Special permissions for caregivers (e.g., babysitters, nannies, grandparents) who are not in a romantic relationship with the parents but need access to certain information.

## 5. Privacy and Security

*   **Emergency Information:** A secure place to store emergency contacts, medical information, and other sensitive data, with granular control over who can access it.
*   **"Incognito" Mode:** A way to browse the calendar without leaving a "read" receipt or other digital footprint, for situations where privacy is paramount.
*   **Data Portability and Deletion:** Clear and easy-to-use tools for exporting or deleting all personal data, in compliance with data privacy regulations like GDPR.

## 6. Community and Social Features

*   **Public or Semi-Public Events:** A way to share events with the wider polyamorous community (e.g., a local meetup, a workshop, a party).
*   **Resource Sharing:** A place to share articles, books, podcasts, or other resources related to polyamory and ethical non-monogamy.
*   **Mentorship Connections:** A feature to help connect experienced polyamorous people with those who are new to the lifestyle, fostering a sense of community and support.
